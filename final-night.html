Monday Night, March 23

The weather is on your side: 10/7°C, partly cloudy, almost no rain, light winds. A decent evening for a walk or one final outdoor moment before you leave.

Option 1: Dublin City Centre

Dublin is the strongest choice for your final night. You get a proper farewell dinner, an evening walk, and a manageable morning drive to the airport. The city has energy on a Monday evening -- not the chaos of a Friday, but enough life to feel like a real goodbye.

Getting to the Airport Tuesday
Dublin city centre to DUB Terminal 2 is about 15 km. OSRM says ~24 minutes in ideal conditions, but morning traffic (especially on the M1/M50 corridor) realistically makes it 30--40 minutes. The M50 toll is EUR 3.80 and automatically billed to rental cars. Leave by 13:00 to be safe.

Monday Afternoon in Dublin

If you're already in Dublin by Monday afternoon (or arrive early), there are several things worth seeing -- especially if you skipped Dublin on Day 1.

Glasnevin Cemetery
Finglas Road — History
The resting place of de Valera, Michael Collins, and Countess Markievicz. Free to walk the grounds. Guided tours available earlier in the day but the grounds themselves are open. A sober, powerful place.
History
Collins Barracks — National Museum
Benburb Street — FREE
"Proclaiming a Republic" exhibition traces the 1916 Rising and independence movement. A quieter, less crowded alternative to Kilmainham Gaol, and completely free. Worth an hour or two.
History
GPO & Witness History Museum
O'Connell Street
The General Post Office was rebel headquarters during the 1916 Easter Rising. The bullet marks are still in the columns. The Witness History exhibition inside is well done and interactive.
1916 Rising
Moore Street
Off O'Connell Street — Walk-By
The final surrender location of the 1916 Rising. Not a museum -- just a street. But if you're at the GPO anyway, it's a two-minute walk to stand where it ended.
History
Kevin & Howlin
Nassau Street — Tweed Shopping
Last chance for Donegal tweed. Ireland's most respected tweed shop. If you didn't make it here on Day 1, Monday afternoon is your final window.
Closes 5:30 PM
Shopping
EPIC The Irish Emigration Museum
CHQ Building, Custom House Quay — ~EUR 17
Interactive museum tracing the Irish diaspora. Near the docks, well-designed, and less crowded than the bigger attractions. A good option if you want something immersive but not heavy.
Interactive
Last Chance
This is your final window for Kilmainham Gaol if you skipped it on Day 1. Check for cancellation tickets at kilmainhamgaolmuseum.ie -- Monday afternoon tours sometimes have openings from day-of cancellations. It's the single most powerful political history site in Ireland, and it's gone once you leave.

Option 2: Near Dublin Airport

The pragmatic choice. Airport hotels around Dublin are generic -- Radisson Blu, Maldron, Clayton -- but they're functional and cheap. You'll be five minutes from the rental return desk. No alarms, no traffic anxiety, no Tuesday morning surprises.

The disadvantage is real, though: spending your last night in Ireland at an airport hotel is like ending a novel mid-sentence. The dinner options near DUB are limited to hotel restaurants and chain pubs. If you've been eating at places like Kai and Ard Bia all week, it'll feel like a letdown.

This option makes the most sense if you're arriving back late on Monday night and just need a bed. Otherwise, Dublin city centre is only 30 minutes further and infinitely better.

Option 3: Stay in Galway

The romantic choice. If you took the slow Connemara route or just weren't ready to leave the west, one more night in Galway gives you another evening of trad music, another walk along the Spanish Arch, another pint at Tigh Neachtain.

But the math is tight. Galway to Dublin Airport is 215 km and realistically takes 2 hours 48 minutes to 2 hours 55 minutes. You need to be at Terminal 2 by 14:00. That means leaving Galway by 10:30 AM at the absolute latest -- and earlier if you want any margin. Tuesday's forecast is rain (14mm), which could add another 15--20 minutes to the drive.

Timing is Tight
Galway → DUB Airport: 215 km, ~2h48--2h55 in good conditions. In Tuesday's rain, budget 3 hours. Add 30 minutes for rental return and shuttle. That means leaving Galway by 10:00--10:30 AM to make a 14:00 airport arrival. You won't sleep in.

Option 4: Somewhere En Route

If you're driving back from Cork or Kerry on Monday, you don't have to push all the way to Dublin. The Dublin-Cork corridor passes through some of Ireland's most interesting medieval towns. Stop in one, have dinner, sleep, and finish the drive Tuesday morning with just an hour or two to go.

Rock of Cashel
Cashel, Co. Tipperary — EUR 8
Dramatic 12th-century ruins perched on a hilltop overlooking the Golden Vale. A cathedral, round tower, and Romanesque chapel. One of Ireland's most iconic silhouettes. The kind of thing you pull over for.
Open 9:00 AM -- 5:00 PM in March
Medieval
Kilkenny
Co. Kilkenny — Medieval Town
The Medieval Mile connects Kilkenny Castle (13th century, free grounds) to St. Canice's Cathedral. In between: narrow lanes, craft shops, and the Smithwick's brewery experience. A proper small Irish city with real character.
Kilkenny to Dublin Airport: ~130 km, about 1h30--1h45 in the morning.
Medieval Craft Beer
Carlow
Co. Carlow — Quiet Stop
Smaller and quieter than Kilkenny, but closer to Dublin. If you just need a bed and an easy Tuesday drive, Carlow is practical. About 85 km / 1h10 to the airport.
En Route Timing
  • Cashel: ~170 km to DUB, roughly 2 hours. Leave by 11:30 AM Tuesday.
  • Kilkenny: ~130 km to DUB, roughly 1h30--1h45. Leave by noon Tuesday.
  • Carlow: ~85 km to DUB, roughly 1h10. Leave by 12:30 PM Tuesday.
  • All of these give you a much more relaxed Tuesday morning than Galway.
