Where the Trip Turns

You're back in Galway. The islands are behind you. Now comes the question that shapes the rest of the trip: how you get to Cork, not whether. Cork and Kinsale are your weekend base, full stop. You've got a rental car, roughly three days left, and a hard deadline: Dublin Airport Terminal 2 by 2:00 PM Tuesday, March 24th for your 4:45 PM Aer Lingus flight home. The only question is what you see on the way south.

Every route below lands you in Cork/Kinsale by Saturday. They just show you a different Ireland along the way.

Route A The Wild Atlantic Way
Galway → The Burren → Cliffs of Moher → Doolin → Ferry to Tarbert → Dingle → Killarney → Kenmare → Cork/Kinsale
This is the route travel writers rave about. Coastal cliffs dropping into the Atlantic, medieval fishing villages, the Dingle Peninsula, and the Ring of Kerry. Two full days of driving with stops at every turn. It's the most you can see before you reach Cork, and you'll earn every mile.
What you gain
  • Cliffs of Moher + the Burren
  • Dingle Peninsula
  • Ring of Kerry (Kenmare side)
  • The full Wild Atlantic Way experience
  • Arrive Cork area Saturday evening
What you miss
  • A lot of time in the car
  • Less time in Cork/Kinsale itself
  • Saturday evening arrival means one full day (Sunday) in Cork before heading to Dublin
Logistics
Driving: ~6-7 hrs total over 2 days, not counting stops. You'll want to stop constantly. Arrive: Cork area Saturday evening. Pace: Tight but doable if you leave Galway early Friday.
The tradeoff
You see the most dramatic coastline in Ireland. But you're in the car a lot, and you arrive in Cork with just Sunday to explore before heading north to Dublin on Monday. If the scenery along the way matters more than time at the destination, this is your route.

Route B The Clare Shortcut
Galway → The Burren → Cliffs of Moher → Doolin → south via Limerick/N20 → Cork/Kinsale
You get the two most iconic sights on the west coast, then cut south to Cork instead of swinging through Kerry. After Doolin, you head inland through Ennis and pick up the N20 motorway south through Limerick to Cork. It's about 2.5 hours from Doolin to Cork once you're on the road. You skip Dingle and the Ring of Kerry, but you arrive in Cork by Friday evening or Saturday morning, giving you a proper weekend there.
What you gain
  • Cliffs of Moher + the Burren
  • Arrive Cork by Friday evening or Saturday morning
  • Full weekend in Cork/Kinsale
  • More relaxed pace
What you miss
  • Dingle Peninsula
  • Ring of Kerry
  • The coastal road south of Clare
Logistics
Driving: ~4 hrs total. Galway to Cliffs (~1.5 hrs with Burren stops), Doolin to Cork (~2.5 hrs via N20). Arrive: Cork Friday evening if you push through, or Saturday morning with a night in Doolin. Note: This route starts the same as Route A. The fork comes at Doolin: go west toward the ferry and Dingle (Route A), or go south toward Limerick and Cork (Route B).
The tradeoff
You get the Cliffs and the Burren but skip everything south of Clare. The payoff is time. An extra day in Cork means you can properly explore the English Market, walk Kinsale's harbor, and eat well without rushing. If you think you'll come back to Ireland someday, Kerry can wait.

Route C The Direct Road
Galway → M6 east → M8 south → (optional: Rock of Cashel) → Cork/Kinsale
The fast option. About 3 hours on the motorway, Galway to Cork. You could spend the morning in Connemara (Pearse's Cottage in Rosmuc opened March 17th for the season, and it's a powerful 1916 site), or have a late breakfast in Galway before hitting the road. Either way, you're in Cork by Friday afternoon with the entire weekend ahead of you.

The Rock of Cashel is right off the M8, about halfway. It's one of Ireland's most striking medieval sites: a cluster of ruins on a limestone outcrop in the middle of Tipperary. Worth a 45-minute stop to stretch your legs and walk among the old walls.

What you gain
  • Maximum time in Cork/Kinsale (arrive Friday afternoon)
  • Rock of Cashel (medieval ruins, right off the motorway)
  • Optional morning: Connemara/Pearse's Cottage or leisurely Galway
  • Most relaxed overall pace
  • Full Friday evening + Saturday + Sunday in Cork
What you miss
  • Cliffs of Moher
  • The Burren
  • Dingle, Ring of Kerry
  • All of the west coast south of Galway
Logistics
Driving: ~3 hrs on motorway (add 45 min for Rock of Cashel stop). Arrive: Cork Friday afternoon. Optional add-on: Morning drive to Pearse's Cottage in Rosmuc (~1 hr from Galway) before heading south. This adds about 2 hours to your day but gets you deep into the Connemara Gaeltacht.
The tradeoff
You skip every cliff and fishing village on the west coast. But you get nearly three full days in Cork and Kinsale, which is enough time to really settle in. The English Market, Charles Fort, Kinsale's harbor walks, proper restaurant dinners without watching the clock. If you'd rather go deep on one place than skim six, this is the call.

Before You Decide

Book Cork accommodation now
Whatever route you pick, you'll need a place in the Cork/Kinsale area for Saturday and Sunday nights. March isn't peak season, but Kinsale B&Bs are small and fill up fast. If you're doing Route A and need a Friday night stop, Doolin or Dingle B&Bs are worth booking now too.
Cork to Dublin on Monday/Tuesday
All three routes end in Cork. On Monday you'll need to head back north to Dublin for your Tuesday flight. Options: drive the car back (~3 hrs on the motorway), train Cork→Dublin Heuston (2.5 hrs, ~€30-50, then taxi to the airport), or fly Cork→Dublin (~30 min, check Aer Lingus/Ryanair, often €30-60). The train is the most reliable. Don't leave this for the last day.
Weather window
The forecast is dry through Sunday, with rain arriving Tuesday. Routes A and B put you on exposed coastal roads and cliff walks on Friday/Saturday, which is perfect timing for the dry spell. Route C gets you to Cork fastest and lets you save outdoor exploring for the dry weekend days. By Tuesday when you're heading to Dublin, you'll be on the motorway and the rain won't matter.
• • •

How to Choose

If this is your only trip to Ireland, do Route A. You'll see the Cliffs of Moher, Dingle, and the Kerry coast before landing in Cork. It's a lot of driving but you won't regret it.
If you want the big sights but also a real weekend in Cork, Route B. You get the Cliffs and the Burren, then cut south and arrive early enough to enjoy Cork properly. Kerry can wait for a return trip.
If you'd rather go deep than go wide, Route C. Three full days in Cork and Kinsale, with a stop at Rock of Cashel and maybe a morning in Connemara. No rushing, no tight logistics, just settling into a place and letting it unfold.
If you're tired from the islands and don't want another big driving day, Route C. The motorway is easy, and you'll be sitting in a Kinsale pub by Friday evening.
