The Drive

You picked up the rental car at ~5:30--6:00 AM. The M6 motorway from Dublin to Galway is about 200 km of straight, well-signed road. Even jet-lagged, this is one of the easiest drives you'll do all week.

208 km Distance
2.5--3 hr Drive Time
€1.90 M6 Toll
6:00 AM Target Departure
Driving Notes
Leave by 6 AM, arrive Galway ~8:30--9:00 AM. The M6 is a motorway the whole way. Two toll plazas: M4 near Enfield (€3.60) and N6 between Ballinasloe and Loughrea (€2.30). Total ~€5.90, cash or card. You'll drive through the midlands -- flat green fields, not much to see from the road. Save your scenery appetite for Connemara.

Optional Stop: Kilbeggan Distillery

Kilbeggan Distillery Experience
Whiskey History
Ireland's oldest licensed distillery, established 1757. Right off the M6 at Junction 5, about one hour from Dublin Airport. Shut down in 1957, revived and distilling again since 2007.
Guided tour: 1.5 hours, €35/person. Walks you through the original mash tuns, oak fermentation vats, and ancient pot stills, ending with a four-whiskey tasting masterclass.
Fill your own bottle: For an extra €35 you can fill a 70cl bottle directly from an aged cask using a valinch (whiskey-drawing tube) and affix a personalized label. This is the thing that makes the stop worthwhile.
Open daily. Check kilbegganwhiskey.com for exact hours -- first tours typically start at 9:30 AM.
Stop or push on?
  • If you stop: Budget 1.5--2 hours. You'll arrive in Galway around 11:00 AM instead of 9:00 AM. You definitely miss the morning ferry to the Aran Islands.
  • If you skip it: Micil Distillery in Galway is your backup whiskey experience, but Kilbeggan's fill-your-own-bottle from the cask is unique. There's nothing else like it on your route.
  • Recommendation: If catching the 10:30 AM ferry is your priority, skip Kilbeggan and come back to it on Day 6 when you're driving east back to Dublin. If the ferry isn't in play for today, stop here on the way out.

Optional Stop: Athenry

Athenry
Medieval Walkabout
The best-preserved medieval walled town in Ireland. Right on the M6, about 30 minutes east of Galway.
Athenry Castle: Anglo-Norman castle from ~1250. Open from March 13, daily 9:30 AM -- 6:00 PM. Admission €5.
Heritage Centre: Opens for the 2026 season on Wednesday March 18 -- your arrival day. Medieval Experience tours available from 10:00 AM.
The song: "The Fields of Athenry" -- the famous Famine ballad you'll hear in every pub this week -- is about this town. Written in the 1970s but set in the 1840s, it tells the story of a man transported to Australia for stealing corn to feed his starving family.
A quick 30-minute walkabout is enough to see the walls and castle exterior. Add 45 min if you go inside.
Timing
Athenry is a minor detour, not a destination. If you're already stopping at Kilbeggan, adding Athenry means arriving in Galway around noon. If you skipped Kilbeggan, a quick Athenry stop still gets you to Galway by ~9:30--10:00 AM.

Galway Morning Options

If you arrive by 9:00 AM and aren't rushing for the ferry, here's what's worth your time before lunch.

Shopping

Standun
Shopping Spiddal, Connemara (20 min west of Galway, on the road to Rossaveel)
Family-run since 1946. The first shop to export Aran sweaters to the US. Huge selection of knitwear, tweed, and Irish crafts in a landmark Connemara building. If you're heading toward the Rossaveel ferry, you'll drive right past it.
Mon--Sat 9:30 AM -- 6:30 PM, Sun 12:00 -- 6:00 PM
O'Maille's (O Maille -- The Original House of Style)
Shopping 16 High Street, Galway
Founded 1938. Handknit Aran sweaters marked with each knitter's name, tweed caps, wool throws. Tailored costumes for the cast of The Quiet Man. One of the last places selling genuinely handknit Aran sweaters.
Heads up: The owners Anne and Ger O Maille announced their retirement in late 2025 after 86 years. Confirm they're still open before making this a destination -- check their Facebook page or call ahead.
If open: typically Mon--Sat, hours vary

Culture

Galway City Museum
History Spanish Arch, Galway
Free admission. Modern building on the banks of the River Corrib overlooking the Spanish Arch. The "Revolution in Galway" exhibition covers the War of Independence and Civil War. Plan 45 min to 1.5 hours.
Tue--Sat 10:00 AM -- 5:00 PM. Closed Mondays. (March 18 is a Wednesday -- you're good.)

Walking

Latin Quarter & Spanish Arch
Walkabout Galway City Centre
The medieval heart of Galway. Narrow streets, buskers, colorful shopfronts. Walk from Eyre Square down Shop Street and High Street to the Spanish Arch and the Long Walk along the harbor. This is the Galway postcard. In March it won't be wall-to-wall tourists -- you'll get to see the real city waking up.
Always open. Best in the morning light.

The Big Question

◆ DECISION POINT: CAN YOU CATCH TODAY'S FERRY?

The Aran Island Ferries winter schedule runs one morning departure from Rossaveel to Inis Mor:

10:30 AM Ferry Departs Rossaveel
~40 min Crossing Time
30 min Galway to Rossaveel
€7 Pier Parking (24 hr)

The math: If you left Dublin Airport at 6:00 AM and drove straight, you'd reach Galway by ~8:30 AM. Rossaveel is 30 minutes further west. That puts you at the pier by ~9:00 AM, giving you 90 minutes to check in. It's tight, but it works.

There's also an evening ferry at 6:00 PM (Sat--Thu, which includes Wednesday). So if you arrive in Galway late morning, you could explore the city and catch the 6:00 PM boat. You'd arrive on Inis Mor at ~6:40 PM, in time for dinner but with no daylight left for sightseeing.

If you make the 10:30 ferry
You'll be on Inis Mor by ~11:10 AM with the whole day ahead of you. That's enough time to rent bikes, cycle to Dun Aonghasa (the prehistoric cliff fort), walk the stone-walled roads, and still catch sunset over the Atlantic.
Book accommodation BEFORE boarding
Do not get on the ferry without a bed confirmed on the island. In winter there are limited options. Port Aran House ($116/night, 9.6 rating on Booking.com) had only 1 room left as of planning. The island is small and you can't just "figure it out" when you arrive.
If you stopped at Kilbeggan
You've missed the 10:30 AM ferry. Your options are the 6:00 PM evening ferry (arrive in the dark) or take the ferry tomorrow morning instead. Tomorrow's 10:30 AM is a much more relaxed plan.
The tradeoff
  • Rush for the ferry: You skip every stop on the drive west. No Kilbeggan, no Athenry, no Galway morning. But you get a full day on the islands, which is the single most memorable experience on this trip.
  • Explore Galway today, ferry tomorrow: You get the Latin Quarter, the museum, maybe Standun. The islands wait until Thursday. This is the less stressful option after a red-eye.
  • Evening ferry: A compromise. See Galway by day, arrive on Inis Mor for dinner and a quiet evening. Full island day Thursday.
Rush for the 10:30 AM Ferry
Drive straight to Rossaveel. No stops. You'll be on Inis Mor by 11:10 AM with the whole day ahead. This requires leaving Dublin by 6:00 AM sharp and pre-booking your island accommodation.
Tight but doable Best for: maximizing island time
Explore Galway Today, Ferry Tomorrow
Take the pressure off. Stop at Kilbeggan if you want. Spend the afternoon walking Galway's Latin Quarter and Spanish Arch. Catch the 10:30 AM ferry Thursday morning, well-rested.
Relaxed pace Best for: jet-lag recovery
Skip the Islands Entirely
Not everyone needs to go to the Aran Islands. If rough seas, tight schedules, or limited island accommodation make it impractical, spend your time in Galway and the Burren instead. You won't regret it.
More flexibility Frees up a full day
