Monday: The Drive North

265 km Cork to Dublin Airport
~3 hrs Via M8 / M7
M8 Motorway the whole way

You've got the car until you hand it back at Dublin Airport on Tuesday. The 265 km drive north is fast motorway, but the route passes three of Ireland's best medieval sites. The real question is whether you drive straight to Dublin for the evening, or break the trip in Kilkenny.

◆ MONDAY DRIVE: STRAIGHT TO DUBLIN OR OVERNIGHT IN KILKENNY?
Option A: Drive Straight to Dublin
Leave Cork after breakfast. Stop at Rock of Cashel and Cahir Castle on the way. Arrive Dublin mid-afternoon. Full evening for dinner, pubs, and last-minute shopping. Your last night in Ireland is a Dublin night.
Leave Cork ~9--10 AM Arrive Dublin ~3--4 PM Full Dublin evening
Option B: Overnight in Kilkenny
Drive halfway. Rock of Cashel and Cahir Castle in the morning, settle into Kilkenny for the afternoon. Walk the Medieval Mile, explore the castle, have a great dinner. Then it's only 1.5 hours to Dublin Airport on Tuesday morning -- the most relaxed departure possible.
Kilkenny overnight 1.5 hrs to airport Tuesday Most relaxed departure
The tradeoff
  • Straight to Dublin: More time for last-chance Dublin sights -- Kilmainham Gaol (check cancellations), Kevin & Howlin, a proper farewell dinner. But it's a longer single stretch of driving.
  • Kilkenny overnight: The most relaxed way to end the trip. You trade a Dublin evening for a medieval town evening. Short drive to the airport Tuesday means no alarms, no stress.

Stops along the M8

Whether you're going all the way to Dublin or stopping in Kilkenny, these three are right on the route and worth your time.

Rock of Cashel
Historic site Tipperary
Iconic hilltop ruins rising from the Tipperary plain. A medieval cathedral, round tower, and Romanesque chapel clustered on a limestone outcrop that's been a seat of power since the 4th century. The Kings of Munster ruled from here for 400 years before handing it to the Church in 1101. You can see it from the motorway, and it's even more impressive up close.
€8 admission. Open 9 AM -- 5 PM. Allow 60--90 minutes.
Cahir Castle
Medieval castle Cahir, Tipperary
One of Ireland's largest and best-preserved medieval castles, built on a rocky island in the River Suir. Norman origins, 12th century. The kind of castle that looks like it should have a drawbridge -- because it does. Less crowded than most heritage sites and genuinely impressive inside. Twenty minutes north of Cashel.
€5 admission. Open daily.
Kilkenny
Medieval city Food scene
Ireland's medieval capital. Walk the Medieval Mile from the castle to the cathedral, past narrow lanes, craft shops, and excellent pubs. Kilkenny Castle (free, OPW-maintained) anchors one end; St. Canice's Cathedral and Round Tower anchor the other. If you're overnighting here, you'll have time to explore properly. If passing through, the Medieval Mile walk takes about an hour including the castle grounds.

If you drive straight to Dublin: Monday afternoon

You'll arrive mid-afternoon with time to explore before dinner. These are your last-chance Dublin sights.

Kilmainham Gaol
Museum Inchicore Road
If you missed it on Day 1, this is your final window. Check for cancellation tickets at kilmainhamgaolmuseum.ie -- they drop between 9:15 and 9:30 AM. The most powerful political history site in Ireland: where the leaders of the 1916 Rising were executed. Worth a last-ditch effort.
Collins Barracks
Museum Free admission
National Museum of Ireland. Excellent 1916 Rising exhibition. Free, no booking needed. A solid option if Kilmainham is sold out, and worth visiting in its own right.
GPO & Glasnevin Cemetery
Historic sites
The GPO on O'Connell Street was rebel headquarters during the 1916 Rising -- bullet holes still in the facade. Glasnevin Cemetery (30 min by bus) is where de Valera, Michael Collins, and the major figures of Irish independence are buried. The museum tour is excellent.
Kevin & Howlin
Tweed 31 Nassau Street
The gold standard for handwoven Donegal tweed since 1936. If you didn't get here on arrival day, this is it. Jackets from ~€450, trousers ~€240. Opposite Trinity College.
Closes 5:30 PM.

Dublin dinner (Monday evening)

Glas
Fine dining Chatham Street
100% plant-based fine dining. One of Dublin's best restaurants, full stop. Not "good for a vegetarian place" -- genuinely outstanding. Seasonal tasting menu. Savannah's kind of spot. Book ahead.
Fully plant-based
Cornucopia
Restaurant Wicklow Street
Vegetarian since 1986. A Dublin institution. Generous portions, wholesome food, no ceremony. More casual than Glas, but reliably good. The kind of place where you order too much and don't regret it.
Fully vegetarian

Middle Eastern-inspired menu with strong vegetarian options. Capel Street is one of Dublin's liveliest food streets -- worth the walk across the Liffey.
Vegetarian options
The Winding Stair
Irish Lower Ormond Quay
River views, a bookshop downstairs, and a menu built around Irish seasonal ingredients. Romantic without trying too hard. A good last-night-in-Ireland dinner.
Vegetarian options

Pub 21 Fleet Street
Over 100 whiskeys. One of Dublin's great traditional pubs -- mahogany, stained glass, no TVs. A proper last-night-in-Ireland dram. Fleet Street, just off Temple Bar.

Tuesday: Getting to the Airport

EI 0107 Aer Lingus
16:45 DUB departure
20:25 JFK arrival
32B7CB Booking reference
US Preclearance -- this is a big deal
Dublin Airport has US Customs and Border Protection in the terminal. You clear immigration and customs before you board. When you land at JFK, you walk straight out like a domestic arrival. No immigration queue, no customs line, no baggage re-check. It's one of the best things about flying Dublin to the US. The catch: it adds 30--60 minutes to your airport time. Build that in.

Target: at Terminal 2 by 14:00 (2 PM). That gives you time for check-in, security, the Preclearance queue, and still a buffer for the unexpected.

Rental car return

Car return process
Fill the tank before you get to the airport -- fuel stations near the rental return are overpriced. Check the car for any new damage and photograph everything: all four sides, odometer, fuel gauge. Follow signs to the rental car return area. After drop-off, take the shuttle to Terminal 2. Allow 30 minutes for the whole process.
Chase Sapphire covers CDW
Your Chase Sapphire provides primary collision damage waiver on international rentals. Decline CDW at the counter. If there's any damage dispute, take photos and file through Chase. Keep your rental agreement and all receipts.
M50 toll
The M50 (Dublin's ring motorway) is barrier-free and camera-based. €3.80, automatically billed to rental cars. No action needed on your part.

Terminal 2 process

Step 1
Check in at Aer Lingus desks or self-service kiosks. Drop bags.
Step 2
Security. Standard screening.
Step 3
US Preclearance. 30--60 minute queue. Passport, customs declaration, brief interview. Once you're through this, you're treated as a domestic arrival at JFK.
Step 4
Duty free. This is AFTER Preclearance, and that matters. Purchases here are treated as domestic when you land at JFK. No liquid restrictions on the plane. This is where you buy the whiskey.
Step 5
Board. Gate opens ~16:15. Wheels up 16:45.
20:25 local
Land at JFK. Walk straight out like a domestic arrival. You already cleared customs in Dublin. Welcome home.
Duty free tip
Because you clear US customs in Dublin, purchases from the duty-free shops after Preclearance are treated as if you bought them domestically. No liquid restrictions on the flight, no customs hassle at JFK. This is the moment to buy that bottle of Redbreast 15 or Green Spot you've been eyeing all week.
