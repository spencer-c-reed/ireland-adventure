You land at Dublin Airport at 4:20 AM. Car rental opens around 6 AM. Head straight for Galway on the M6 — mindless motorway driving, perfect for jet-lagged brains. You'll have all morning and early afternoon in Galway before heading south through the Burren to Doolin.

Galway: morning & lunch

You have about 4 hours here — enough to wander the Latin Quarter, eat well, and soak up the atmosphere without rushing. Everything is walkable from Eyre Square.

Galway Woollen Market
Knitwear 21 High Street • 9am-9pm
Traditional hand-loomed Aran knits, tweed, lace, and textiles. Right in the Latin Quarter. The replacement for O'Maille's (which sadly closed in 2024 after 86 years).
Charlie Byrne's Bookshop
Bookshop Middle Street • Mon-Thu 9-6, Fri 9-8, Sat 9-6, Sun 12-6
Legendary independent bookshop with 100,000+ titles in a labyrinthine layout. New, secondhand, rare, and Irish interest. You could lose an hour here easily.
Thomas Dillon's Claddagh Gold
Since 1750 Jewellers Quay Street
Ireland's oldest jewellers. Has a small free Claddagh Museum inside with the oldest known Claddagh rings on display. Quick 15-minute stop. The business changed hands only once, in 1920 — during the War of Independence.
Spanish Arch & the Long Walk
16th century Romantic
Walk through the 16th-century Spanish Arch onto the Long Walk — the prettiest street in Galway. Colorful terraced houses along the water, looking out across Galway Bay.
Galway City Museum
Museum Free admission
Right next to the Spanish Arch. Small but excellent local history, archaeology, and art. Free. A good 30--45 minute visit.

Where to eat in Galway

For lunch, pick one of these. All are in the Latin Quarter within a few minutes' walk.

Ard Bia at Nimmo's
Restaurant Spanish Arch, riverside
Gorgeous setting right on the river at Spanish Arch. Seasonal menu with strong vegetarian dishes. Candlelit stone walls, water outside. One of Galway's best.

The Burren: Galway to Doolin

Between Galway and Doolin, you drive through the Burren — a lunar landscape of cracked limestone stretching to the horizon. It looks like another planet. Nothing prepares you for it. Take the R477 coastal road for Galway Bay views, then cut inland through the heart of the Burren.

Corcomroe Abbey
13th century Near Bellharbour • Free, always open
A Cistercian monastery with beautiful carved stonework, tucked into the Burren hills. Usually uncrowded. The access road looks like a farm road but it's correct. Allow 20-30 minutes.
Burren Perfumery
Perfumery Carran • 10am-5pm daily
Handmade perfumes and organic skincare made from Burren wildflowers. Free admission, herb garden, working factory you can watch. The tea rooms just reopened for the season (March 17). Savannah will love this. Slight detour, adds ~15 minutes.
Poulnabrone Dolmen
Ancient site The Burren R480 • Free
A 5,800-year-old portal tomb sitting in the middle of the Burren limestone. Older than the pyramids. Free, just pull over. Five-minute walk from the road (uneven ground). One of those sights that makes you recalibrate what "old" means.
Caherconnell Stone Fort
Ancient fort R480, right next to Poulnabrone • 10am-4pm
A 1,000-year-old ring fort with guided tours and sheepdog demonstrations. Combine with Poulnabrone since they're right next to each other. Small admission fee.
Burren Smokehouse
Food Lisdoonvarna • 10am-4pm daily
Free visitor centre with their "Taste the Atlantic" smoked salmon experience. Award-winning smoked salmon — good to pick up provisions. Right on the route before Doolin.

Doolin evening: dinner & trad music

You're sleeping in the trad music capital of Ireland tonight. The village is three pubs and a post office. In late March it's quieter than summer, but Wednesday night should still have sessions. Check with Sheedy's when you check in — they'll know who's playing where.

Gus O'Connor's Pub
Trad music Pub
The most famous trad music pub in Ireland. Sessions most nights. Stone floors, open fire, pints of Guinness. If you only go to one pub in Doolin, make it this one.
McGann's
Trad music Pub
Smaller, more intimate than Gus O'Connor's. Often has afternoon sessions. Good pub food — toasties, soup, chowder.
McDermott's
Trad music Pub
The third of the Doolin trinity. More modern feel. Live music most evenings. If the other two are packed, this one will have room.
Oar Restaurant
Fine dining Michelin-listed • Wed-Sun 5:30-9pm
The best food in Doolin, named Best Restaurant in Ireland by YesChef. Modern Irish fine dining with local produce. Always has a vegetarian option. Near Sheedy's. Book by phone: +353 65 704 7990.
Vegetarian options
Sunset at Doonagore Castle
A 16th-century tower on a hilltop above Doolin. Spectacular sunset spot over the Atlantic and Aran Islands. Short drive from the village. Sunset around 6:25 PM in mid-March. Exterior only, always accessible.
More dinner options
The pubs all serve food (chowder, toasties, fish & chips). Doolin Cafe has good vegetarian options and fresh-baked goods. Or ask Sheedy's — they'll know what's best tonight.

Morning: Cliffs of Moher

Cliffs of Moher
Sea cliffs 214m (702 ft)
Sea cliffs dropping straight into the Atlantic. €8 (~$9) entry includes the visitor centre. Walk south past O'Brien's Tower for the best views and fewer people. The cliff walk toward Hag's Head is magnificent if the weather cooperates. Allow 1--2 hours.
March weather at the cliffs
Wind can be brutal. Bring layers and something windproof. The visitor centre is good shelter if it's lashing rain. The cliffs are actually more dramatic in rough weather, as long as you're dressed for it.
◆ ROUTE CHOICE: FERRY OR LIMERICK?
Shannon Ferry (scenic)
Doolin → Killimer → ferry → Tarbert → Tralee → Dingle. More scenic: coastal Clare, 20-min boat ride, Kerry coastline. But requires timing the hourly ferry. ~3h+ total.
€22.50 (~$25) single, car + passengers Every hour on the hour, 7am-7pm
Via Limerick (faster)
Doolin → Ennis → Limerick → Tralee → Dingle. More predictable, slightly faster (~2h50m), no extra cost. More motorway driving.
No ferry to time ~10 min shorter
The Connor Pass
The highest mountain pass in Ireland. Narrow, dramatic, with views down to Brandon Bay on one side and the Dingle Peninsula on the other. Go slow, use passing places. This is the moment the trip shifts from "getting somewhere" to "being somewhere."

Dingle afternoon & evening

You arrive mid-afternoon with time to wander. Dingle is a Gaeltacht area where Irish is the everyday language. Small enough to walk end-to-end in 15 minutes, but the food scene is remarkable.

Solas Tapas & Wine
Tapas Michelin Bib Gourmand • Strand Street
Fresh, beautifully presented small plates perfect for sharing. Great wine list. Ideal if you don't want a heavy meal after a day on the road. Call to confirm Thursday opening: +353 87 993 2116. Book ahead.

Dingle evening: pubs & music

Neligan's
Trad music Main Street
Live music 7 nights a week, 364 days a year. Run by a musical family. Free dance class Thursdays at 8pm — that's your night. Seats by the stove on cold evenings.
Dick Mack's
Pub Leather shop Green Street
Half pub, half leather shop. Own brewery on-site. Hollywood Walk of Fame-style plaques outside (Sean Connery, Julia Roberts, etc.). The most famous Dingle pub.
An Droichead Beag
Trad music Lower Main Street
Nightly trad sessions from 9pm in a building from the 1700s. Gets packed — arrive early for a seat near the musicians. Dingle's only late-night venue.

Friday March 20: Slea Head Drive & Cork

Your last morning on the Wild Atlantic Way. Make it count.

38 km Slea Head loop
2.5--3.5 hrs With stops
Clockwise Avoids tour buses
~2.5 hrs Dingle to Cork

Morning: coffee, then Slea Head Drive

Bean in Dingle
Coffee Green Street • Mon-Sat 8am-4pm, closed Sun
#1 rated coffee shop in Ireland (TripAdvisor). Family-run, they roast their own beans on-site. House blend "An Fear Marbh" (The Sleeping Giant). Homemade cakes and pastries. Start your morning here.
Optional: Dingle Distillery
75-minute guided tour with tastings (whiskey, gin, vodka). Tours at 10am, 11:30am, 2pm, 4pm. €25 (~$27) per person. Book at dingledistillery.ie. The 10am slot works before a late-morning Slea Head departure.

A 38 km circular route around the tip of the peninsula, starting and ending in Dingle town. Drive clockwise to avoid meeting tour buses head-on. March advantage: you'll have it largely to yourselves. Allow 3.5-4 hours with stops.

Ventry Beach
Beach 8km from Dingle • Free
First stop on the loop. A 3km-long beach backed by dunes, often overlooked by people rushing to the headland. Worth a quick stop, especially if the light is good.
Dunbeg Fort
Iron Age ~580 BC • Book at dunbeagfort.com
Iron Age promontory fort perched on the cliff edge. Visitor centre with AV display, then a fenced walkway to the fort (inner area closed due to cliff erosion). Dramatic setting.
Fahan Beehive Huts
Ancient site Farm
2,000-year-old stone beehive huts on a working farm. €4 entry. You can hold a baby lamb. That alone is worth the stop.
Coumeenoole Beach
Beach Romantic
One of the most photographed spots on the drive. Filming location for Ryan's Daughter. Short walk down to the beach. Even in March, striking.
Dunmore Head
Viewpoint Romantic
The westernmost point of mainland Ireland. Next stop west: North America. Short walk from the car park to the headland. Genuinely one of those "edge of the world" moments.
Dunquin Pier
Scenic Free, always accessible
Iconic winding walkway zigzagging down the cliff to the pier below. Views of the Blasket Islands. One of the most photographed spots in Kerry. (The Blasket Island ferry doesn't run in March.)
Gallarus Oratory
7th-century chapel
A remarkably preserved early Christian chapel built entirely from dry-stacked stone, shaped like an inverted boat. Still watertight after 1,300 years. One of the most intact early medieval buildings in Ireland.
Blasket Centre
Museum Dunquin
Museum about the Great Blasket island community, evacuated in 1953. Three classics of Irish autobiography came from a community of fewer than 200 people. The building sits on the cliff overlooking the abandoned island.
Louis Mulcahy Pottery
Ceramics Ballyferriter • 10am-5:30pm daily
Handcrafted ceramics by one of Ireland's most respected potters. Browse the workshop and shop. The upstairs Ri Fern Cafe (Tue-Sun 10am-5pm) makes a good lunch stop on the drive.
Star Wars was here
The beehive huts on Slea Head Drive were a filming location for Star Wars: The Last Jedi. Keep an eye out for the signs.

Lunch in Dingle, then drive to Cork

Head back into Dingle for lunch -- Solas is a good quick option -- then point the car east.

The direct route: Dingle → Tralee (N86) → Killarney (N22) → Cork City (N22). About 2.5 hours total. Good roads after Tralee. Straightforward driving.

Optional: Killarney National Park
If you have time, Killarney is right on the way. Muckross House gardens (free, beautiful) or Ladies View (famous viewpoint, just pull over on the N71) make good 30-minute breaks without a detour. Don't do the full Ring of Kerry -- no time, and you don't need to.

Evening: Arrive Cork

Arrive Cork ~5--6 PM. Your friends are waiting. Settle in, rest up. The anniversary weekend starts now.
