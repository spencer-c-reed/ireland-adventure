Wednesday March 18: Dublin to Galway

You land at Dublin Airport at 4:20 AM. By 6:00--6:30, you'll have the rental car and be ready to go. The question is whether you go straight west or spend the morning in Dublin first.

Kevin & Howlin tweed (31 Nassau Street, opens 10 AM -- Donegal tweed jackets ~€450). GPO on O'Connell Street (1916 bullet holes in the facade). Leave Dublin ~1--2 PM, arrive Galway ~4 PM.
Leave Dublin ~1--2 PM Arrive Galway ~4 PM
Option B: Straight to Galway
On the M6 by 6:30 AM. In Galway by 9 AM. A whole day to explore the Latin Quarter, walk the Long Walk, eat well, settle in. You'll hit Dublin on the way home Monday anyway.
Leave Dublin ~6:30 AM Arrive Galway ~9 AM
The tradeoff
  • Dublin morning: Kilmainham Gaol is the most powerful political history site in Ireland. Kevin & Howlin is the gold standard for Donegal tweed. But you arrive Galway late afternoon with limited time there.
  • Straight to Galway: A full day to explore. You arrive rested enough to wander. The M6 motorway is mindless driving -- good for jet-lagged brains. And you'll pass through Dublin again on Monday.
Kilmainham Gaol tickets
Tickets release 28 days in advance and sell out fast. For March 18, those dropped on February 18. Your best shot is cancellation tickets released each morning between 9:15 and 9:30 AM on the official site. Only buy from kilmainhamgaolmuseum.ie. If sold out, Collins Barracks (free, no ticket needed) has an excellent 1916 Rising exhibition. You'll pass through Dublin again Monday, so don't stress.

Galway afternoon & evening

Galway's Latin Quarter is a tight grid of colorful shop fronts, buskers, and restaurants that punches far above its weight. You could spend a full day here or just an evening -- either way, the vibe is infectious.

Charlie Byrne's Bookshop
Bookshop Middle Street
Legendary independent bookshop with 35,000+ titles across two floors. New, secondhand, rare, and Irish interest. You could lose an hour here easily.
O'Maille's
Traditional clothing Since 1938
Traditional Irish clothing -- Aran sweaters, tweed caps, hand-knit scarves. The real deal, not tourist tat. Family-run for almost 90 years.
Spanish Arch & the Long Walk
16th century Romantic
The Spanish Arch is a 16th-century structure on the River Corrib. Walk through it and you're on the Long Walk -- the prettiest street in Galway. Colorful terraced houses along the water, looking out across Galway Bay. Great for photos, great for a stroll.
Galway City Museum
Museum Free admission
Right next to the Spanish Arch. Small but excellent collection of local history, archaeology, and art. Free. A good 30--45 minute visit.

Thursday March 19: Galway to Cliffs to Doolin to Dingle

The big day. You're covering the most ground today, but it's all spectacular -- lunar limestone, towering sea cliffs, trad music pubs, a car ferry across the Shannon, and a mountain pass into Dingle at sunset.

1.5 hrs Galway to Cliffs
€8 Cliffs entry
20 min Shannon Ferry
~3 hrs Doolin to Dingle (via ferry)

Morning: The Burren & Cliffs of Moher

Drive south from Galway through the Burren -- a lunar landscape of cracked limestone stretching to the horizon. It looks like another planet. Nothing prepares you for it.

Poulnabrone Dolmen
Ancient site The Burren
A 5,800-year-old portal tomb sitting in the middle of the Burren limestone. Older than the pyramids. Free, just pull over. Five-minute walk from the road. One of those sights that makes you recalibrate what "old" means.
Cliffs of Moher
Sea cliffs 214m (702 ft)
Sea cliffs dropping straight into the Atlantic. €8 entry includes the visitor centre. Walk south past O'Brien's Tower for the best views and fewer people. The cliff walk toward Hag's Head is magnificent if the weather cooperates. Allow 1--2 hours.
March weather at the cliffs
Wind at the cliffs can be brutal. Bring layers and something windproof. The visitor centre is good shelter if it's lashing rain. Don't let bad weather stop you going -- the cliffs are actually more dramatic in rough weather, as long as you're dressed for it.

Lunchtime: Doolin

Doolin is three pubs and a post office -- and somehow one of the most famous music villages in Ireland. In high season, every pub has trad sessions nightly. In late March, it's quieter but you'll still find music, especially on a Thursday night.

Gus O'Connor's Pub
Trad music Pub
The most famous trad music pub in Ireland. Sessions most nights. Stone floors, open fire, pints of Guinness. If you only go to one pub in Doolin, make it this one.
McGann's
Trad music Pub
Smaller, more intimate than Gus O'Connor's. Often has afternoon sessions. Good pub food -- toasties, soup, chowder. A nice spot to warm up before the drive south.
McDermott's
Trad music Pub
The third of the Doolin trinity. More modern feel. Live music most evenings. If the other two are packed, this one will have room.
Doolin Cafe
Cafe Doolin village
The best proper food in the village if you want more than pub grub. Good vegetarian options, fresh-baked goods, coffee that's actually good. A solid lunch stop.
Vegetarian options

Afternoon: The Shannon crossing to Dingle

The Killimer--Tarbert car ferry crosses the Shannon estuary and saves you 130 km of driving versus going inland through Limerick. This is the smart route south.

Tralee. Pick up the N86 toward the Connor Pass.

The Connor Pass
The highest mountain pass in Ireland. Narrow, dramatic, with views down to Brandon Bay on one side and the Dingle Peninsula on the other. Go slow, use passing places. This is the moment the trip shifts from "getting somewhere" to "being somewhere."

Dingle evening

Drop your bags, walk the harbor, breathe. You're on the tip of the Dingle Peninsula, in a Gaeltacht area where Irish is the everyday language. The town is small enough to walk end-to-end in 15 minutes, but the food scene is remarkable.

Elegant but unfussy, with an imaginative approach to local Kerry ingredients. The menu rotates seasonally and always includes strong vegetarian options. One of Dingle's best dinners.

Friday March 20: Slea Head Drive & Cork

Your last morning on the Wild Atlantic Way. Make it count.

38 km Slea Head loop
2.5--3.5 hrs With stops
Clockwise Avoids tour buses
~2.5 hrs Dingle to Cork

Morning: Slea Head Drive

A 38 km circular route around the tip of the peninsula, starting and ending in Dingle town. Drive clockwise to avoid meeting tour buses head-on. March advantage: you'll have it largely to yourselves.

Fahan Beehive Huts
Ancient site Farm
2,000-year-old stone beehive huts on a working farm. €4 entry. You can hold a baby lamb. That alone is worth the stop.
Dunmore Head
Viewpoint Romantic
The westernmost point of mainland Ireland. Next stop west: North America. Short walk from the car park to the headland. Genuinely one of those "edge of the world" moments.
Gallarus Oratory
7th-century chapel
A remarkably preserved early Christian chapel built entirely from dry-stacked stone, shaped like an inverted boat. Still watertight after 1,300 years. One of the most intact early medieval buildings in Ireland.
Blasket Centre
Museum Dunquin
Museum about the Great Blasket island community, evacuated in 1953. Three classics of Irish autobiography came from a community of fewer than 200 people. The building sits on the cliff overlooking the abandoned island.
Star Wars was here
The Dingle Peninsula was a filming location for Star Wars: The Force Awakens. The dramatic coastal landscape doubled for the planet where Luke Skywalker was found. Keep an eye out for the signs.

Lunch in Dingle, then drive to Cork

Head back into Dingle for lunch -- the Wren's Nest or Solas are both good quick options -- then point the car east.

The direct route: Dingle → Tralee (N86) → Killarney (N22) → Cork City (N22). About 2.5 hours total. Good roads after Tralee. Straightforward driving.

Optional: Killarney National Park
If you have time, Killarney is right on the way. Muckross House gardens (free, beautiful) or Ladies View (famous viewpoint, just pull over on the N71) make good 30-minute breaks without a detour. Don't do the full Ring of Kerry -- no time, and you don't need to.

Evening: Arrive Cork

Arrive Cork ~5--6 PM. Your friends are waiting. Settle in, rest up. The anniversary weekend starts now.
