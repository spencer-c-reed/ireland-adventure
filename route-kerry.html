Getting There

From Doolin or the Clare coast, you have two main entry points into Kerry. Drive south to Dingle (about 2.5 hours via the N86) or to Killarney (about 2 hours via Limerick and the N21). Dingle is the more remote, atmospheric choice. Killarney is the more practical hub with trains back to Dublin.

~2.5 hr Doolin to Dingle
~2 hr Doolin to Killarney
179 km Ring of Kerry loop

Dingle Peninsula

Dingle is a colorful harbor town on the western tip of Kerry -- a Gaeltacht area where Irish is the everyday language. It's small enough to walk end to end in fifteen minutes, but the food scene punches far above its weight. The town sits at the start of the Slea Head Drive, one of Ireland's most spectacular coastal loops.

Slea Head Drive

A 38 km circular route around the tip of the peninsula, starting and ending in Dingle town. You'll pass beehive huts, Blasket Islands views, ancient forts, and the landscape used in Star Wars: The Force Awakens. Drive it clockwise to avoid meeting tour buses head-on.

Allow 2.5--3.5 hours
The drive itself takes under an hour, but you'll want to stop constantly. Key stops: Fahan Beehive Huts (19 souterrain and 18 standing clochans, EUR 4 entry -- you can also hold a baby lamb on the farm), Dunmore Head (westernmost point of mainland Ireland), Gallarus Oratory (a remarkably preserved 7th-century chapel), and the Blasket Centre (museum honoring the island community evacuated in 1953).

March advantage
Dingle in late March is off-peak. You'll have the Slea Head Drive largely to yourselves, and accommodation that books out months ahead in summer will have availability. The weather won't be warm, but the light on the Atlantic is extraordinary.

Ring of Kerry & Killarney

The Ring of Kerry is a 179 km loop through some of Ireland's most dramatic mountain and coastal scenery. It's a full-day drive if you do the whole thing. But if time is short, concentrate on the Killarney National Park highlights -- they're the best parts anyway.

Killarney National Park

Ireland's first national park. Free to enter, open year-round. 10,236 hectares of lakes, mountains, and ancient oak forest -- the only remaining native woodland of its kind in Ireland.

Muckross House & Gardens
Historic house Gardens
A Victorian mansion at the heart of the national park with extensive gardens and views of Killarney's lakes. The gardens are free; the house is by guided tour. The Traditional Farms are open from March onward. No pre-booking needed -- buy tickets on arrival.
Open daily 9 AM -- 6 PM. Free parking. 6 km from Killarney town.
Gap of Dunloe
Mountain pass Scenic walk
A dramatic glacial valley between the MacGillycuddy Reeks and Purple Mountain. You can walk it (the Wishing Bridge is a 30-minute round trip; the full gap is 11 km, about 2.5 hours one way) or drive it. In March, with fewer crowds, driving is actually feasible -- the narrow road is usually packed with walkers and jaunting cars in summer. Free parking at Kate Kearney's Cottage, 15 minutes from Killarney.
Ladies View
Viewpoint N71 road
One of Ireland's most famous viewpoints, looking down over the Upper Lake and the surrounding mountains. Named for Queen Victoria's ladies-in-waiting, who admired the view during a royal visit in 1861. Free, right on the main road. Just pull over.
Ring of Kerry time check
The full 179 km Ring of Kerry loop takes a solid 4--5 hours of driving, plus stops. If you have a full day, it's a beautiful circuit. If you only have half a day, skip the full ring and focus on Killarney National Park, the Gap of Dunloe, and Ladies View. You'll see the best scenery without the time commitment.

Kenmare

An elegant small town at the head of Kenmare Bay, roughly an hour south of Killarney on the N71. Kenmare is the natural overnight stop between Kerry and Cork -- and the food is excellent.

What's Next: Cork

Cork is your weekend base -- that's non-negotiable. From Kerry, the road south through West Cork is one of the most beautiful stretches of the trip.

◆ GETTING TO CORK FROM KERRY
Via Kenmare & the West Cork Coast
From Kenmare, it's about 2 hours to Kinsale via the N71 through Bantry and Bandon. You'll cross the Caha Pass, pass through Michael Collins country, and arrive in Ireland's food capital. The scenic route.
~2 hr drive Best for: scenery + history
Direct from Killarney to Cork City
The N22 from Killarney to Cork City is about 90 km and takes roughly 1h15. Skip West Cork and Kinsale, go straight to the city. You can always drive to Kinsale (25 min) from Cork the next day.
~1h15 drive Best for: if it's getting late
Which route to Cork?
  • Via Kenmare/West Cork: More scenic, but the roads are narrow and slow. Budget the full 2 hours. Worth it if you have the daylight.
  • Direct from Killarney: Faster and on better roads. Doesn't mean you miss Kinsale -- just means you visit it from Cork on Saturday or Sunday instead of arriving through it.
Time check
You need to be in the Cork/Kinsale area by Saturday. If it's Saturday and you're still in Kerry, take the direct Killarney-Cork route (1h15) and skip the scenic West Cork drive. If it's Friday, you have time for the Kenmare/Bantry route.
