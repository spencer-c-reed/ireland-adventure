The single most important thing about this day: Dublin has US Preclearance. You clear US customs and immigration before you board in Dublin. That means when you land at JFK, you walk straight out like a domestic arrival. No immigration queue, no customs line. It's wonderful on the JFK end, but it adds serious time at Dublin. Returning the Rental Car

No matter where you're coming from, the car return process at Dublin Airport works the same way. Allow 30 minutes from pulling into the rental village to stepping into the terminal.

Before you arrive

  • Fill petrol at the station near the airport rental village (it's on the approach road — follow signs). Most rental agreements require a full tank on return.
  • Check the car for new damage. Walk around it. Take photos of all four sides, the roof, and the wheels. Do this before you pull into the return bay.
  • Check the boot, glove box, under seats, door pockets — nothing left behind.
  • Remove any rubbish. Leave the car the way you found it.

At the rental village

Follow signs for "Car Rental Return" as you approach Dublin Airport. The rental car village is separate from the terminals. You'll pull into your rental company's return bay, hand over the keys, and get a shuttle bus to Terminal 2. The shuttle runs frequently (every 5--10 minutes), but don't count on it being instant.

Insurance Note
You declined the rental company's CDW because the Chase Sapphire covers international rental car insurance. If there's any damage dispute at return, don't sign anything admitting fault. Take photos, note the agent's name, and file the claim through Chase when you get home. The Sapphire's coverage is primary for international rentals.
M50 Toll
The M50 (Dublin's ring road) has a camera-based toll. No stopping, no toll booth — it photographs your plate. The toll is EUR 3.80 for rental cars, and the rental company will typically auto-charge it plus an admin fee. You'll see it on your final rental statement. Nothing you need to do.

Dublin Airport Terminal 2

Working backward from the 16:45 departure, here's the target timeline:

~14:00 — Arrive at Terminal 2
Recommended arrival time. This gives you nearly 3 hours before departure, which sounds like a lot until you factor in Preclearance. Check in at the Aer Lingus desks (or use the self-service kiosks if you've checked in online).
~14:15 — Security screening
Standard airport security. Laptops out, liquids in a bag. Usually moves quickly at T2.
~14:30 — US Preclearance
This is the big one. You go through US Customs and Border Protection right here in Dublin. It's a full US immigration checkpoint: passport scan, photo, fingerprints, questions about your trip. The queue can take 30--60 minutes depending on how many US-bound flights are departing around the same time. Be patient. Have your passports ready.
~15:00 — 15:30
Through Preclearance. You're now in the departures area. Duty free shopping, food, pints. Relax. The hard part is done.
~16:15
Boarding begins for EI 0107.
16:45
Departure. Dublin to JFK, arriving 20:25 local time. You walk off the plane and straight out of the airport. No queues. You're home.
Duty Free Tip
Duty free in Dublin T2 is AFTER US Preclearance. That means anything you buy — Irish whiskey, wool, chocolate, whatever — you carry onto the plane. When you land at JFK, you're treated as a domestic arrival. No customs declaration for duty free goods purchased after Preclearance. This is the time to buy that bottle of Redbreast 15 or a Donegal tweed scarf.

Tuesday's downpour is Ireland's parting gift: a reminder that you got incredibly lucky with the weather, and a reason not to feel bad about leaving.
