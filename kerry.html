Wednesday: Dublin to Kerry

You land at Dublin Airport at 4:20 AM. By 5:30 or 6:00, you'll have the rental car. From here, you've got a choice to make: spend the morning in Dublin, or get on the road and be in Dingle by lunch.

◆ FIRST CALL OF THE TRIP: DUBLIN OR STRAIGHT TO KERRY?
Option A: Dublin Morning First
Spend the morning in Dublin. Breakfast, Kilmainham Gaol (if you can get cancellation tickets), Kevin & Howlin tweed shop on Nassau Street, the GPO. Leave Dublin by 1--2 PM. Arrive Dingle late afternoon/evening.
Leave Dublin ~1--2 PM Arrive Dingle ~5--6 PM
Option B: Drive Straight to Kerry
On the road by 6 AM. M7 to Limerick, then N21 to Tralee and N86 to Dingle. You'll be walking the harbor and eating lunch in Dingle while the Dublin option is still shopping for tweed.
Leave Dublin ~6 AM Arrive Dingle ~10 AM
The tradeoff
  • Dublin morning: Kilmainham Gaol is the most powerful political history site in Ireland. Kevin & Howlin is the best Donegal tweed shop in the country. If you're doing a one-way car to Cork, this is your only Dublin time.
  • Straight to Kerry: More time on the peninsula. You'll arrive rested enough to explore Dingle in daylight. You can always hit Dublin on a future trip; you can't get this week's weather back.

Kilmainham tickets
Tickets release 28 days in advance and sell out fast. For March 18, those dropped on February 18. Your best shot is cancellation tickets released each morning between 9:15 and 9:30 AM on the official site. Only buy from kilmainhamgaolmuseum.ie -- third-party tickets aren't valid. If Kilmainham is sold out, Collins Barracks (free, no ticket needed) has an excellent 1916 Rising exhibition 15 minutes away.

The drive: Dublin to Dingle

The route is straightforward. M7 southwest to Limerick (about 2.5 hours, mostly motorway). Then N21 to Tralee and N86 over the Connor Pass into Dingle (about 1.5 hours more, increasingly scenic). Total: roughly 4 hours of driving. The last stretch over the Connor Pass is narrow and dramatic -- mountains on both sides, views down to the coast. It's the moment the trip shifts from "getting there" to "being there."

Driving on the left
If this is your first time, the M7 motorway is forgiving -- wide, well-signed, mostly straight. Roundabouts are the main hazard: go clockwise, yield to the right. The roads narrow significantly after Tralee. Caffeine is not optional, especially if you've been up since a red-eye.

Dingle evening

However you get here, you'll arrive in a colorful harbor town on the western tip of Kerry. Dingle is a Gaeltacht area -- Irish is the everyday language on street signs and in shops. The town is small enough to walk end to end in fifteen minutes, but the food scene punches far above its weight. After the drive, drop your bags, stretch your legs along the harbor, and eat well.

Thursday: The Dingle Peninsula

This is your big day on the peninsula. The Slea Head Drive is one of the most spectacular coastal loops in Ireland, and in late March you'll have it largely to yourselves. After that, if time and energy allow, you can dip into the Killarney National Park highlights in the afternoon.

March advantage
Dingle in late March is off-peak. The Slea Head Drive, which is bumper-to-bumper with tour buses in July, will be yours. The light on the Atlantic is extraordinary this time of year -- low-angled, constantly shifting. Bring a camera.

Morning: Slea Head Drive

A 38 km circular route around the tip of the peninsula, starting and ending in Dingle town. Drive it clockwise to avoid meeting tour buses head-on. The drive itself takes under an hour, but you'll want to stop constantly. Allow 2.5--3.5 hours with stops.

38 km Slea Head loop
2.5--3.5 hrs With stops
Clockwise Avoids tour buses
Fahan Beehive Huts
Ancient site Farm
19 souterrain and 18 standing clochans (stone beehive huts), some over 2,000 years old. The family that owns the farm charges €4 entry -- and you can hold a baby lamb. That alone is worth the stop.
Dunmore Head
Viewpoint Romantic
The westernmost point of mainland Ireland. Stand here and the next stop west is North America. On a clear day, the Blasket Islands seem close enough to swim to. It's a short walk from the car park to the headland -- and genuinely one of those "edge of the world" moments.
Gallarus Oratory
7th-century chapel
A remarkably preserved early Christian chapel built entirely from dry-stacked stone, shaped like an inverted boat. It's been standing here since around 700 AD, still watertight. One of the most intact early medieval buildings in Ireland.
Blasket Centre
Museum Dunquin
A museum honoring the island community of the Great Blasket, evacuated by the Irish government in 1953 when the population became too small to sustain itself. The islanders produced an extraordinary literary tradition -- three classics of Irish autobiography came from a community of fewer than 200 people. The building itself sits on the cliff overlooking the abandoned island.
Star Wars was here
The Dingle Peninsula was used as a filming location for Star Wars: The Force Awakens. The dramatic coastal landscape doubled for the planet where Luke Skywalker was found. Keep an eye out for the signs.

Afternoon option: Killarney National Park highlights

If you finish Slea Head by early afternoon and still have energy, the Killarney area is about an hour east of Dingle. You don't need to do the full Ring of Kerry -- just cherry-pick the best parts.

Killarney National Park
National Park Free admission
Ireland's first national park. 10,236 hectares of lakes, mountains, and ancient oak forest -- the only remaining native woodland of its kind in Ireland. Free to enter, open year-round.
Muckross House & Gardens
Historic house Gardens
A Victorian mansion at the heart of the national park. The gardens are free and beautiful; the house is by guided tour. The Traditional Farms are open from March onward. No pre-booking needed.
Open daily 9 AM -- 6 PM. Free parking. 6 km from Killarney town.
Gap of Dunloe
Mountain pass Scenic walk
A dramatic glacial valley between the MacGillycuddy Reeks and Purple Mountain. The Wishing Bridge is a lovely 30-minute round trip walk. The full gap is 11 km (about 2.5 hours one way). In March, with fewer crowds, driving the narrow road is actually feasible -- it's usually packed with walkers and jaunting cars in summer. Free parking at Kate Kearney's Cottage, 15 minutes from Killarney.
Ladies View
Viewpoint Romantic
One of Ireland's most famous viewpoints, looking down over the Upper Lake and the surrounding mountains. Named for Queen Victoria's ladies-in-waiting, who admired the view during a royal visit in 1861. Free, right on the N71. Just pull over.
Ring of Kerry time check
The full Ring of Kerry loop is 179 km and takes 4--5 hours of driving, plus stops. If you have a full day, it's a beautiful circuit. But if time is short after Slea Head, skip the full ring and focus on Killarney National Park, the Gap of Dunloe, and Ladies View. You'll see the best scenery without the time commitment.

Thursday evening: back to Dingle for dinner, or start making your way toward Kenmare if you're ready to set up for the drive to Cork on Friday.

Friday: Kerry to Cork

Cork is your weekend base -- that's non-negotiable. Friday is about making your way south, with a couple of route options depending on where you woke up and how much driving you want to do.

Kenmare: the natural overnight stop

If you stayed in or near Killarney on Thursday night, Kenmare is about an hour south on the N71. It's an elegant small town at the head of Kenmare Bay, and the food is excellent. Even if you're just passing through, it's worth a stop for lunch.

Getting to Cork

◆ ROUTE DECISION: SCENIC OR DIRECT?
Via Kenmare & the West Cork Coast
From Kenmare, take the N71 through Bantry and Bandon. You'll cross the Caha Pass, drive through Michael Collins country, and arrive in Cork via Kinsale. The roads are narrow and slow, but the scenery is spectacular. Budget the full 2 hours.
~2 hrs to Kinsale Scenic but narrow roads Best for: if you have the daylight
Direct from Killarney to Cork City
The N22 from Killarney to Cork City is about 90 km and takes roughly 1h15. Better roads, faster, straightforward. You can always drive to Kinsale (25 min) from Cork the next day.
~1h15 to Cork City Good roads, easy drive Best for: if it's getting late
Which route to Cork?
  • Via Kenmare/West Cork: More scenic, but the roads are narrow and slow. Worth it if you have the daylight and the energy. The Caha Pass alone is worth the detour.
  • Direct from Killarney: Faster and on better roads. Doesn't mean you miss Kinsale -- just means you visit it from Cork on Saturday or Sunday instead of arriving through it.
Time check
If it's Friday and you're still in Dingle, take the direct Killarney-Cork route (N22, about 1h15 from Killarney to Cork City). Don't try to squeeze in the scenic West Cork coast -- you'll arrive in Cork after dark and miss the whole point. The scenic route works best if you're leaving from Kenmare with a full afternoon ahead of you.

However you get there, the goal is simple: arrive in Cork on Friday afternoon or evening, settled and ready for the weekend. Cork is where the trip shifts gears -- from driving and exploring to slowing down, eating well, and celebrating your anniversary.
