Six Days, One Trip of a Lifetime

You land at Dublin Airport at 4:20 AM on Wednesday, March 18th. You fly home from Dublin at 4:45 PM on Tuesday, March 24th. In between: six days, a rental car, and an island that rewards people who don't over-plan.

The route is set. This guide gives you the facts, the stops, and the options that actually matter. Everything else, you'll figure out when you get there.

The Shape of the Trip

Your week has three acts.

Act I: Wednesday -- Friday
The Wild Atlantic Way. Dublin to Galway, south through the Burren to the Cliffs of Moher and Doolin, across the Shannon by ferry, over the Connor Pass into Dingle, then the Slea Head Drive before heading to Cork. Three days of the best Ireland has to offer.
Act II: Saturday -- Sunday
Cork & Kinsale. Your anniversary weekend with friends. Cork's English Market, Kinsale's harbor and star fort, Cafe Paradiso for a dinner you'll talk about for years. This is the anchor.
Act III: Monday -- Tuesday
Getting home. Drive the M8 back toward Dublin with stops at Rock of Cashel, Cahir Castle, and Kilkenny along the way. At the airport by 2 PM Tuesday.

The car
Round-trip rental, Dublin Airport to Dublin Airport. You have the car the whole week -- it gets you to Kinsale (25 min from Cork), on day trips with friends, and back to Dublin on Monday via the M8 with stops. Request an automatic. Chase Sapphire covers CDW (decline it at the counter).

The Route

Galway → Cliffs → Doolin → Dingle → Cork
Three days down the Wild Atlantic Way. Galway's Latin Quarter and pubs on Wednesday. The Burren, Cliffs of Moher, and Doolin's trad music on Thursday, then the Shannon ferry and Connor Pass into Dingle. Slea Head Drive on Friday morning, then south to Cork for the weekend.
Wed: Dublin → Galway Thu: Cliffs → Doolin → Dingle Fri: Slea Head → Cork
Jet Lag Reality Check
You land after a red-eye at 4:20 AM. Even if you slept on the plane, you'll hit a wall by early afternoon. The M6 motorway to Galway is mindless driving -- 2.5 hours, follow the signs. Good for jet-lagged brains. Be honest about how you'll feel at 9 AM after no sleep.
Before you go
Car pickup: Dublin Airport rental desks open 5--6 AM. Request an automatic. Chase Sapphire covers international rental car insurance (primary coverage) -- decline the CDW at the counter.

Parking: Purchase Park 2 Fly ($15/day, free shuttle, valet + car wash). Book at pp2f.com for March 18--24. Terminal 7 (Aer Lingus).

Cafe Paradiso, Cork: Book now. Ireland's best vegetarian restaurant, and your anniversary dinner destination. Saturday or Sunday evening.
