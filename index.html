Your Adventure Begins

You land at Dublin Airport at 4:20 AM on Wednesday, March 18th, bleary-eyed after a red-eye from JFK. You have six full days and five nights before your return flight from Dublin at 4:45 PM on Tuesday, March 24th.

Ireland is small but dense with things worth seeing. You can't do everything, but the magic is in choosing well. This guide maps every fork in the road, tells you what you'd gain and what you'd miss, and flags the moments where a decision needs to be made before the window closes.

Jet Lag Reality Check
You're landing after a red-eye. Even if you slept on the plane, you'll hit a wall by early afternoon. Day 1 is about pacing yourselves -- don't try to cram Dublin AND drive to Galway AND catch a ferry. Pick one thing and do it well.

The Shape of the Trip

At the highest level, your week has three acts:

Act I: Wed--Thu
West. Get to Galway and the Aran Islands. This is the wild, raw, Gaeltacht heart of Ireland -- cliffs, stone walls, Irish-speaking islanders, Atlantic gales.
Act II: Fri--Sun
South to Cork. All roads lead to Cork for the weekend. The question is which road: the full Wild Atlantic Way through Clare and Kerry, the Clare shortcut via Limerick, or the direct motorway. You'll be based in the Cork/Kinsale area Saturday and Sunday.
Act III: Mon--Tue
Return. Work your way back to Dublin Airport by 2 PM Tuesday. Where you wake up Monday morning depends on every choice you've made before.

First Decision

It's 4:20 AM. You've cleared customs. Your rental car counter opens between 5:00 and 6:00 AM. What do you do with your first morning in Ireland?

◆ DECISION POINT: YOUR FIRST MORNING
Drive West to Galway
Pick up the car and hit the M6. You'll be in Galway by mid-morning, with time to catch the afternoon ferry to the Aran Islands or explore the city. Stop at Kilbeggan Distillery on the way.
2.5--3 hr drive Best for: maximizing island time
Spend the Morning in Dublin
Ease into Ireland. Get breakfast, walk through the city half-awake. Hit Kilmainham Gaol, the tweed shops, the GPO. Drive west after lunch -- there's an 18:00 evening ferry to Inis Mor, so you don't have to sacrifice island time.
Leave Dublin by 1 PM for evening ferry Best for: jet lag recovery + history
What's at stake
  • Drive west now: You gain a full afternoon in Galway or even catch the ferry to Inis Mor today. But you skip Dublin entirely until the last day (and you may not have time then either).
  • Stay in Dublin: You get Kilmainham Gaol (the single most powerful political history site in Ireland) and Kevin & Howlin (best tweed shop in the country). You'll arrive in Galway late -- but there's an 18:00 evening ferry to Inis Mor (Sat-Thu), so if you leave Dublin by 1 PM and drive straight to Rossaveel, you could still reach the island tonight.
  • You'll pass through Dublin on the return. The trip goes Cork → Dublin for Tuesday's flight, so you'll have Monday evening or Tuesday morning in the city if you want it.
Time-sensitive
Kilmainham Gaol tickets: Guided tours only, tickets are released 28 days in advance and sell out fast. For a Wednesday March 18 visit, tickets would have been released on Feb 18. Check kilmainhamgaolmuseum.ie right now -- there may be cancellations, or they may be gone. If they're gone, that tilts toward driving west.
Good to know
Car pickup: Dublin Airport rental desks typically open at 5:00--6:00 AM. You'll need to request an automatic specifically. Budget had availability from ~$124 for the week. The Chase Sapphire covers rental car insurance internationally, so decline the CDW at the counter.
