Six Days, One Big Decision

You land at Dublin Airport at 4:20 AM on Wednesday, March 18th. You fly home from Dublin at 4:45 PM on Tuesday, March 24th. In between: six days, a rental car for the whole trip, and an island that rewards people who don't over-plan.

This guide gives you the facts, the tradeoffs, and the choices that actually matter. Everything else, you'll figure out when you get there.

The Shape of the Trip

Your week has three acts. Act II is fixed. Acts I and III are yours to shape.

Act I: Wednesday -- Friday
The road to Cork. Three days to get from Dublin Airport to Cork. You can go west through Galway and the Aran Islands, south through Kerry and the Dingle Peninsula, or take the direct route through Ireland's medieval heartland. Each is a different trip. The only rule: be in Cork by Friday evening.
Act II: Saturday -- Sunday
Cork & Kinsale. Your anniversary weekend. Cork's English Market, Kinsale's harbor and star fort, Cafe Paradiso for a dinner you'll talk about for years. This is the anchor. Everything else flows around it.
Act III: Monday -- Tuesday
Getting home. Drive the M8 back toward Dublin with stops at Rock of Cashel, Cahir Castle, and Kilkenny along the way. At the airport by 2 PM Tuesday.

The car
Round-trip rental, Dublin Airport to Dublin Airport. You have the car the whole week -- it gets you to Kinsale (25 min from Cork), on day trips with friends, and back to Dublin on Monday via the M8 with stops. Request an automatic. Chase Sapphire covers CDW (decline it at the counter).

The Decision: Your Route to Cork

You have three days (Wednesday through Friday) to get from Dublin Airport to Cork. Three routes, each with a different character. Pick the one that matches your energy after a red-eye.

◆ CHOOSE YOUR ROUTE
The West Coast
Galway, the Aran Islands, Cliffs of Moher. Three days of Atlantic drama -- Irish-speaking islands, 200-metre sea cliffs, trad music pubs in Galway and Doolin. The most ambitious route. The most driving. The biggest payoff if the weather cooperates.
Wed: Dublin → Galway Thu: Aran Islands Fri: Cliffs → Cork
Kerry & Dingle
The Dingle Peninsula, Ring of Kerry, Killarney National Park. Ireland's most stunning driving roads -- mountain passes, hidden beaches, a stone oratory that's stood since the 7th century. Romantic, dramatic, and the food in Dingle rivals anywhere on the island.
Wed: Dublin → Kerry Thu: Dingle / Ring of Kerry Fri: Kerry → Cork
The Slow South
Dublin's political history in the morning, medieval Kilkenny and the Rock of Cashel on the drive down, Cork by Thursday. Three or four full days in the Cork area instead of two. Less driving, more living. The best choice if you want to settle in rather than sprint across the country.
Wed: Dublin sights → drive south Thu: Arrive Cork Fri: Bonus Cork day
What's at stake
  • West Coast: You see the wildest parts of Ireland. The Aran Islands are unlike anything else in the country -- a treeless moonscape of limestone and dry-stone walls where people still speak Irish as their first language. But you won't arrive in Cork until Friday evening, and a bad weather day can scramble the plan.
  • Kerry: The best single day of driving in Ireland is the Slea Head Drive on the Dingle Peninsula. If scenery is what you came for, this is the route. Cork arrival Friday afternoon.
  • Slow South: You trade the wild west coast for time. Kilmainham Gaol on Day 1 means you don't have to squeeze it in at the end. Three or four days to explore Cork, Kinsale, and West Cork at anniversary pace. Least driving, most relaxation.
Jet Lag Reality Check
You land after a red-eye at 4:20 AM. Even if you slept on the plane, you'll hit a wall by early afternoon. The West Coast route handles this by putting you on the M6 motorway (mindless driving, 2.5 hours, follow the signs to Galway). Kerry works if you leave Dublin by late morning. The Slow South lets you ease in with a coffee and a walk before deciding anything. There's no wrong choice, but be honest about how you'll feel at 9 AM after no sleep.
Before you go
Car pickup: Dublin Airport rental desks open 5--6 AM. Request an automatic. The Chase Sapphire covers international rental car insurance (primary coverage), so decline the CDW at the counter.

Kilmainham Gaol: Guided tours only. Tickets released 28 days ahead and sell out fast. Check kilmainhamgaolmuseum.ie for cancellations. If you can get a Wednesday morning slot, take it -- it's the most powerful political history site in Ireland. If it's sold out, that tilts toward the West Coast route (you'll pass through Dublin on the way home).

Cafe Paradiso, Cork: Book now. Ireland's best vegetarian restaurant, and your anniversary dinner destination. Saturday or Sunday evening.
