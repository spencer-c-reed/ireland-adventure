You've chosen to stay in Dublin for the morning. Smart. You just crossed the Atlantic on a red-eye, and the idea of immediately navigating Irish roundabouts on the left side of the road while half-asleep is... not ideal. Dublin won't blow you away the way the West will, but it has things you can't get anywhere else: the best political history sites in the country, and the best tweed shopping.

Your timeline
4:20 AM: Land at Dublin Airport. 5:00--6:00 AM: Pick up rental car. ~6:30 AM: Breakfast. 9:00 AM--12:30 PM: Dublin sights and shopping. 12:30--1:30 PM: Lunch. ~2:00 PM: Drive west to Galway.

Jet Lag Breakfast (5--7 AM)

You'll have the car by 5:30 or 6. The airport is about 30 minutes from Dublin city centre. You've got two options: eat near the airport before driving in, or drive straight to the city and find something there. Most Dublin cafes don't open until 8, so truly early options are limited.

Near the Airport

Dublin Airport Terminal Eateries
Airport • Open from ~5 AM
Not glamorous, but you're not glamorous at 5 AM either. Harvest Market in the terminal has decent coffee and pastries. Eat here to take the edge off, then get a proper breakfast in the city once places open.
Open from early morning, landside and airside options

City Centre -- Early Openers

Keogh's Cafe
Breakfast 1--2 Trinity Street, Dublin 2
Opens at 6:30 AM on weekdays -- one of the earliest independent cafes in the city. Right next to Trinity College. Good coffee, full Irish breakfast, and the kind of no-fuss spot where you can stare at the wall for twenty minutes while your brain catches up with your body. Popular with locals heading to work.
Mon--Sat 6:30 AM -- 6:30 PM
Vegetarian options available
Cornucopia
Breakfast 19/20 Wicklow Street, Dublin 2
Fully vegetarian/vegan since 1986. Opens at 8 AM on weekdays, so this works if you take it slow and arrive in the city around 7:30. Self-service counter with hot dishes, soups, and pastries -- all made fresh. Savannah would love this place. Two floors, relaxed atmosphere, good coffee.
Wed: 8:00 AM -- 7:00 PM
100% Vegetarian & Vegan
The Bretzel Bakery
Bakery 1 Lennox Street, Portobello, Dublin 8
A Dublin institution since 1870. Jewish-Irish bakery known for sourdough, challah, and excellent pastries. Opens at 7 AM on weekdays. About a 10-minute drive south of the city centre, near the Grand Canal. If you're heading toward Kilmainham anyway, it's roughly on the way.
Mon--Fri from 7:00 AM, Sat from 8:00 AM
Vegetarian options available
Realistic expectation
If you land at 4:20 and clear customs and car rental by 5:30--6:00, you'll reach the city centre around 6:30. Keogh's will just be opening. If you want Cornucopia or Bretzel, you could park near Trinity, walk around half-asleep for an hour, and eat at 8. Or just grab something at the airport and save the sit-down for later.

Dublin Highlights (9 AM -- 12:30 PM)

You have about three and a half hours. You can't do all of these, but you can do two or three depending on how deep you go. Here they are, roughly in order of how much they'd matter to you.

1. Kilmainham Gaol

Kilmainham Gaol Museum
History Inchicore Road, Kilmainham, Dublin 8
The single most powerful political history site in Ireland. This is where the leaders of the 1916 Rising were held and executed -- Pearse, Connolly, Plunkett (who married Grace Gifford in the chapel hours before his execution). Also held Parnell, de Valera, and countless others going back to 1796. The guided tour walks you through the cells, the yards, and the execution courtyard. It's devastating and unforgettable.
Access is by guided tour only. Tours take about 60 minutes, plus 30 minutes for the museum. Arrive 15 minutes early. Tickets are €8 per adult.
Generally open from 9:30 AM. Tours run throughout the day.
Tickets may be sold out
Kilmainham releases tickets exactly 28 days in advance. For Wednesday March 18, those tickets dropped on February 18 and likely sold out within hours. But: cancellation tickets are released online each morning between 9:15 and 9:30 AM. If you're serious about going, refresh kilmainhamgaolmuseum.ie at 9:15 sharp. Only buy from the official OPW site -- third-party tickets aren't valid.
If Kilmainham is sold out
National Museum of Ireland -- Collins Barracks is free, 15 minutes from Kilmainham, and houses the Proclaiming a Republic: The 1916 Rising exhibition. It tells the same story through personal belongings, uniforms, bullets, and first-aid kits from the rebels, British soldiers, and civilians caught in the crossfire. It's not as visceral as standing in the execution yard, but it's genuinely excellent and you can walk in without a ticket. Hours: Tue--Sat 10 AM -- 5 PM. Benburb Street, Dublin 7.

2. The Tweed Shops

Dublin has the two best Donegal tweed shops in the country. If you're doing a one-way car rental ending in Cork, this is your only shot at these. Both are in the Grafton Street area, walkable from each other in about 5 minutes.

Kevin & Howlin
Tweed 31 Nassau Street, Dublin 2
The gold standard. Founded in 1936, specializing exclusively in handwoven Donegal tweed since 1973. Jackets run around €450, trousers around €240. They also do made-to-measure if you want to go deep. Old-world shop with incredibly knowledgeable staff who'll walk you through the different weights and weaves. Opposite Trinity College.
Mon--Sat 10:00 AM -- 4:00 PM
Celtic Tweed
Tweed 28 South Anne's Lane (off Grafton Street), Dublin 2
Award-winning tailors and craftsmen. Handmade tweed and linen clothing, produced in their own Dublin workshop. More contemporary cuts than Kevin & Howlin while still using traditional fabrics. They also run guided tours of their workshop if you're interested in seeing how the garments are made.
Mon--Sat 9:00 AM -- 5:00 PM
One-way rental warning
If you're doing a one-way car to Cork, this is your only Dublin time. Stock up on tweed. You won't find shops like these in Galway or along the coast. O'Maille's in Galway carries Aran knits and some tweed, but for proper Donegal tweed tailoring, Dublin is the place.

3. GPO Witness History

GPO Museum
History O'Connell Street, Dublin 1
The General Post Office was the rebel headquarters during the 1916 Rising -- where Pearse read the Proclamation of the Republic from the front steps. The museum inside is interactive and well-produced, walking you through the week of the Rising from the rebels' perspective. The building itself still has bullet holes in the facade. Allow about 90 minutes.
Book tickets in advance online to save €2--5. Walk-ups accepted but advance booking recommended.
Mon--Sat 10:00 AM -- 5:00 PM (last admission 4:00 PM). Closed Sundays.

4. Trinity College & Book of Kells

Book of Kells Experience
Culture Trinity College, College Green, Dublin 2
The 9th-century illuminated manuscript, plus the Long Room -- one of the most beautiful libraries in the world. Timed entry tickets required, and March is off-peak so Wednesday morning slots should be available. Takes about 90 minutes total. If you're already at Kevin & Howlin on Nassau Street, Trinity's front gate is a 2-minute walk.
Tickets around €18--25 for adults. Book at visittrinity.ie.
Wed in March: 9:30 AM -- 5:00 PM

5. Glasnevin Cemetery

Glasnevin Cemetery
History Finglas Road, Glasnevin, Dublin 11
Ireland's national cemetery. De Valera, Michael Collins, Countess Markievicz, Brendan Behan, Daniel O'Connell -- they're all here. Guided history tours run at 11:30 and 1:30. The O'Connell Tower tour lets you climb Ireland's tallest round tower for a view over the city. About 3 km north of the city centre (bus 40 or 140 from O'Connell Street). This one is harder to fit in if you're also doing Kilmainham and the tweed shops, since it's on the opposite side of the city.
Book tours in advance at dctrust.ie.
Grounds: daily 9 AM -- 5 PM. Visitor Centre: Wed--Sun 10 AM -- 5 PM. First tour at 11:30.

Lunch (12:30 -- 1:30 PM)

Eat before you drive. You'll be on the M6 for two and a half hours, and hunger plus jet lag plus left-side driving is a bad combination.

Glas Restaurant
Lunch 15/16 Chatham Street, Dublin 2
Ireland's premier vegetarian restaurant, just off Grafton Street. Won Best Vegetarian Restaurant (RAI Awards 2023) and Best Sustainable Restaurant (RAI Awards 2025). Also 100% gluten-free. Creative, beautifully plated dishes -- this is proper fine-dining vegetarian, not a hippie buffet. Lunch is a relaxed, set-menu affair. Perfect for Savannah. Reserve via OpenTable or call +353 1 672 4534 (they take a €20/person deposit, deducted from the bill).
Wed lunch: 12:00 PM -- 3:30 PM
100% Vegetarian, Vegan & Gluten-Free
Cornucopia
Lunch 19/20 Wicklow Street, Dublin 2
If you didn't hit Cornucopia for breakfast, it's a great lunch option too. Self-service counter with rotating hot dishes, soups, salads, and excellent baked goods. More casual and faster than Glas -- good if you want to eat and get on the road. Family-run since 1986.
Wed: 8:00 AM -- 7:00 PM
100% Vegetarian & Vegan

Head West

◆ AFTERNOON: DRIVE TO GALWAY
Hit the M6 to Galway
Two and a half hours on the motorway, most of it flat midlands. Consider a stop at Kilbeggan Distillery (halfway) to break up the drive. Arrive Galway around 4:30--5:00 PM.
~2.5 hr drive ~210 km Motorway the whole way
Galway arrival timing
If you leave Dublin by 1 PM, you can reach Rossaveel by 4:00--4:30 PM -- in time for the 18:00 evening ferry to Inis Mor (runs Sat-Thu in winter). That means you don't have to sacrifice island time by staying in Dublin. Leave by 2 PM and you'll arrive in Galway around 4:30--5:00 PM instead -- too late for the ferry, but perfect for an evening in Galway: Micil Distillery tour, O'Maille's for Aran knits, and dinner on the west coast.
Driving tips
You'll be driving on the left for the first time, probably exhausted. The M6 motorway is forgiving -- it's wide, well-signed, and mostly straight. Roundabouts are the main hazard. Go clockwise. Yield to the right. Take it slow the first few times. Caffeine is not optional.

The Tradeoff

What you gain by spending the morning in Dublin
  • Kilmainham Gaol or Collins Barracks -- the most important political history sites in the country
  • Kevin & Howlin and Celtic Tweed -- the best Donegal tweed shopping you'll find anywhere
  • A gentler start to the trip. You ease into jet lag instead of fighting it on a motorway at dawn.
  • GPO, Trinity, Glasnevin -- you can sample as much or as little of Dublin as you want
What you miss
  • If you leave by 1 PM, you can still catch the 18:00 evening ferry to Inis Mor -- but you'll arrive on the island after dark with no time to explore until morning
  • If you leave after 2 PM, you miss the ferry and need to wait until Thursday's 10:30 AM sailing
  • If the weather window for the Aran Islands is Wednesday and turns bad Thursday, a late Dublin departure means you'll have missed it
